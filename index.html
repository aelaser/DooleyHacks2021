About us:

DooleyHacks is Emory University’s revitalized Hackathon, presented by our premiere tech club HackEmory. While HackEmory has been focusing on smaller community-level projects in recent years, we are have been launching large-scale events, namely our 2020 Hackathon “NextGen” last spring. With a vision of gathering the community of people interested in various technologies, HackEmory believes that this is truly the perfect era for establishing the “next generation” of Emory’s focus on innovation through DooleyHacks.

Emory prides itself in its well-rounded liberal arts education, with students adopting a multidisciplinary approach to their academics and striving to create a curriculum in which they may satisfy their intellectual curiosity. A large portion of students at Emory undertake a health or business track, but many of them express a strong interest in the tech world -- evident through previous Hackathons and campus-wide events.

HackEmory is excited to foster the community from the ground up by bringing together students of varying interests into our neoteric facility -- all features that collectively allow DooleyHacks to stand out.

FAQ:

  • What is a Hackathon?

      A hackathon is best described as an “invention marathon”. Anyone who has an interest in technology attends a hackathon to learn, build & share their creations over the course of a weekend in a relaxed and welcoming atmosphere

  • What if I've never been to a Hackathon, Mini or otherwise?

      No worries! Prior to the event, we'll be sending out our Hacker guide, detailing the ways we think you can make the most out of your day. During the event, the DooleyHacks team will be available for questions. As always, feel free to reach out to us at hello@dooleyhacks.com if you have any other questions!

  • Who can apply?

      Anyone who is 18 or older (Yes, we check IDs!) and a currently enrolled college student with a valid student ID is eligible for our event.

  • What should I bring?

      All you'll need is a photo ID for check-in, your laptop and charger for hacking, and a good attitude!

  • How do I form or join a team? Do I need a team?

      We encourage everybody to get in teams of up to 4 Hackers, and we will help facilitate this with a team-forming event at the start of the Hackathon, as well as a slack channel for team-formation that all participants will be added to prior to the event. However, teams are not necessary, so feel free to work individually

  • Is there reimbursement for travel?

      Unfortunately, we will be unable to provide any sort of travel reimbursement for this years event, but hopefully next year we will be able to provide this service for our first full-fledged Hackathon!

  • Do I need to stay the whole time?

      As college students ourselves, we understand how busy things can get. You're welcome to come and go from the event as you please, but make sure to keep your registration badge on you at all times, without it you won't be re-admitted to the venue!

  • I have no idea how to code. Can I still attend?

      Absolutely! A Hackathon is primarily a learning experience, and we welcome applicants regardless of experience in tech, so long as you're willing to learn. To help bring you up to speed, we will have mentors at the event who can help you along the way.

  • Why is it called DooleyHacks?

      Claire E. Dooley is Emory's unofficial mascot, and our Lady of Misrule. We feel that she truly embodies the feel of the Hackathon, and as her motto states, "Presidents may come, presidents may go; professors may come, professors may go; students may come, students may go; but Dooley goes on forever!".
